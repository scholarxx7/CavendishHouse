A Legacy Etched in Time

The historical journey of Cavendish House since 1928.

The Story of Cavendish

Patna Science College was established in 1927 as a premier institution for higher education in science. To accommodate the brilliant minds flocking to this new center of learning, the need for state-of-the-art residential facilities was paramount. Thus, Cavendish House was conceived.

Named after Henry Cavendish, the brilliant British natural philosopher and scientist known for discovering hydrogen and weighing the Earth, the hostel was designed to embody the spirit of scientific inquiry. The architecture reflects the colonial style of the era, characterized by high ceilings, thick walls for natural cooling, and large windows that allow ample light—symbolizing the enlightenment of knowledge.

On November 15, 1928, the hostel was officially inaugurated by the then Viceroy of India, Lord Irwin. Since that historic day, Cavendish House has stood as a silent witness to the changing tides of history, from the pre-independence era to the modern digital age, all while nurturing generations of scientists, bureaucrats, and leaders.

Historical Timeline

1

1927

Foundation of Patna Science College, setting the stage for a new era of scientific education in Bihar.

2

Inauguration Day. Cavendish House is officially opened by Viceroy Lord Irwin, dedicated to the students of Science.

3

1940s-50s

The Golden Era. The hostel houses some of the brightest minds who would go on to rebuild independent India's scientific infrastructure.

4

1960s-70s

The Era of Genius. Dr. Vashishtha Narayan Singh associated with the college, inspiring a generation of mathematicians.

5

2003

Platinum Jubilee Celebrations of Patna Science College, marking 75 years of excellence.

6

Present Day

Cavendish House continues to be a merit-based abode, equipped with modern amenities like Wi-Fi while preserving its heritage structure.
